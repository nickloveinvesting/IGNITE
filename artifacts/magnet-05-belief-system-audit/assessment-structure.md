# Belief System Audit - 8-Question Assessment

## ASSESSMENT DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

**Behavior-Based vs. Belief-Based Questions**
- 7 behavioral questions (what you actually did)
- 1 emotional response question (what triggers you)
- Questions span multiple domains: career, money, health, relationships
- Focus on last 30 days (recent, specific, memorable)
- Third-person perspective in one question to reduce bias

**Scoring Framework**
- Each question maps to 2-3 belief pattern indicators
- Patterns identified through cross-domain consistency
- Results show primary pattern + secondary influences
- Personalized report based on dominant pattern

---

## THE 8 QUESTIONS

### QUESTION 1: Trade-Off Under Pressure (Career/Health Domain)

**Question:**
"In the last 30 days, when you faced a deadline or high-pressure work situation, what did you do first?"

**Options:**
- A) Cancelled personal commitments (gym, social plans, self-care) to focus on work
- B) Asked for help or delegated to protect my time
- C) Worked longer hours but maintained my non-negotiables
- D) Avoided the situation until the last possible moment

**Belief Pattern Indicators:**
- **A = Worthy-When Achiever** (worth earned through sacrifice)
- **B = Healthy Operator** (balanced beliefs)
- **C = Fixed-Capacity Believer** (zero-sum thinking)
- **D = Perfectionist Procrastinator** (paralysis by analysis)

**Why This Question:**
Reveals true priorities under constraint. Shows if worth is tied to sacrifice, if boundaries exist, or if avoidance is the default pattern.

---

### QUESTION 2: Financial Decision Pattern (Money Domain)

**Question:**
"Thinking about a significant financial decision you made (or avoided) in the last 3 months—what drove that choice?"

**Options:**
- A) Fear of losing what I already have
- B) Excitement about what I could gain
- C) What others would think of the decision
- D) Waiting for more information/certainty before deciding

**Belief Pattern Indicators:**
- **A = Scarcity Operator** (loss-aversion dominant)
- **B = Abundance Mindset** (opportunity-focused)
- **C = Approval Seeker** (external validation)
- **D = Perfectionist Procrastinator** (analysis paralysis)

**Why This Question:**
Money decisions reveal core beliefs about scarcity vs. abundance, risk tolerance, and whether decisions are self-directed or other-directed.

---

### QUESTION 3: Pattern Recognition Across Domains (Multi-Domain)

**Question:**
"What pattern keeps repeating in your life despite your awareness of it?"

**Options:**
- A) Starting strong, then quitting when it gets hard
- B) Choosing security over what I actually want
- C) Overcommitting and burning out
- D) Waiting for permission or validation before acting
- E) Achieving goals but feeling unfulfilled anyway

**Belief Pattern Indicators:**
- **A = Evidence Collector** (past failure predicts future)
- **B = Scarcity Operator** (fear-based decisions)
- **C = Worthy-When Achiever** (worth through productivity)
- **D = Approval Seeker** (external locus of control)
- **E = Fixed-Capacity Believer** (can't have it all)

**Why This Question:**
Self-identified patterns reveal self-awareness while showing which belief system is creating the loop. Recurring patterns indicate subconscious belief systems.

### QUESTION 4: Relationship Priority Test (Relationship Domain)

**Question:**
"When someone close to you (partner, friend, family) expressed disappointment in a choice you made, what was your first instinct?"

**Options:**
- A) Justify my decision—I made the right choice
- B) Question my decision—maybe I got it wrong
- C) Feel guilty—I should have considered their needs first
- D) Feel defensive—they don't understand the pressure I'm under

**Belief Pattern Indicators:**
- **A = Healthy Operator** (secure in decisions)
- **B = Approval Seeker** (external validation needed)
- **C = Approval Seeker** (people-pleasing)
- **D = Worthy-When Achiever** (self-justification through sacrifice)

**Why This Question:**
Tests whether self-worth is internal or external. Reveals if approval is needed for decision confidence or if boundaries are secure.

### QUESTION 5: Success Attribution (Career/Identity Domain)

**Question:**
"When you think about your biggest professional success in the last year, what immediately comes to mind first?"

**Options:**
- A) The recognition or validation I received
- B) The problem I solved or value I created
- C) How hard I worked or how much I sacrificed
- D) How lucky I was with timing or circumstances
- E) I can't think of a success worth mentioning

**Belief Pattern Indicators:**
- **A = Approval Seeker** (external validation focus)
- **B = Healthy Operator** (intrinsic motivation)
- **C = Worthy-When Achiever** (worth through effort)
- **D = Scarcity/Imposter** (discounting own agency)
- **E = Evidence Collector** (focus on failure)

**Why This Question:**
Projective question using automatic first response. Reveals whether identity is tied to external approval, effort, or intrinsic value creation.

---

### QUESTION 6: Violation of Principles (Values Domain)

**Question:**
"Describe a time in the last 6 months when you violated your most important principle or value. What made that situation different?"

**Options:**
- A) Financial pressure—I needed the money/security
- B) Others were depending on me—I couldn't let them down
- C) I was exhausted—I didn't have energy to maintain standards
- D) Everyone else was doing it—it felt normal in that context
- E) I haven't violated my core principles

**Belief Pattern Indicators:**
- **A = Scarcity Operator** (fear overrides values)
- **B = Approval Seeker** (others' needs before own values)
- **C = Worthy-When Achiever** (burnout from unsustainable standards)
- **D = Approval Seeker** (conformity pressure)
- **E = Perfectionist/Denial** (unrealistic self-view or avoidance)

**Why This Question:**
Forces acknowledgment of contradictory evidence. Reveals true hierarchy of beliefs when values conflict with other needs.

---

### QUESTION 7: Third-Person Perspective (Reduces Bias)

**Question:**
"If someone who knows you well (partner, close friend, colleague) described the gap between what you say you value and what you actually prioritize through your actions, what would they say?"

**Options:**
- A) "You say you value health but you always sacrifice it for work"
- B) "You say you value authenticity but you change based on who you're with"
- C) "You say you value financial freedom but you won't take risks"
- D) "You say you value rest but you feel guilty when you're not productive"
- E) "There's no gap—you live your values consistently"

**Belief Pattern Indicators:**
- **A = Worthy-When Achiever** (worth through work)
- **B = Approval Seeker** (identity shifts for acceptance)
- **C = Scarcity Operator** (fear prevents action)
- **D = Worthy-When Achiever** (rest as earned, not needed)
- **E = Healthy Operator OR denial**

**Why This Question:**
Third-person perspective reduces defensive bias. People are more honest about gaps when framing it as "what others would say."

---

### QUESTION 8: Emotional Trigger Identification (Emotional Response)

**Question:**
"What situation in the last month triggered your strongest negative emotional reaction (anger, shame, anxiety, inadequacy)?"

**Options:**
- A) Someone questioned my competence or expertise
- B) I missed a financial opportunity or lost money
- C) Someone succeeded where I've been struggling
- D) I failed to meet my own standards or expectations
- E) I had to choose between competing priorities (work vs. family, money vs. values)

**Belief Pattern Indicators:**
- **A = Approval Seeker** (identity threatened by external judgment)
- **B = Scarcity Operator** (loss triggers core fear)
- **C = Evidence Collector** (comparison confirms limiting belief)
- **D = Perfectionist Procrastinator** (self-judgment)
- **E = Fixed-Capacity Believer** (zero-sum worldview)

**Why This Question:**
Emotional intensity indicates proximity to core beliefs. Strong reactions reveal which belief is most deeply held and protected.

## SCORING SYSTEM

### Step 1: Pattern Frequency Calculation

Count frequency of each belief pattern across all 8 questions:

| Belief Pattern | Frequency Count |
|----------------|----------------|
| Scarcity Operator | ___ |
| Approval Seeker | ___ |
| Perfectionist Procrastinator | ___ |
| Worthy-When Achiever | ___ |
| Fixed-Capacity Believer | ___ |
| Evidence Collector | ___ |

### Step 2: Determine Primary Pattern

**Primary Pattern:** Highest frequency score (3+ occurrences indicates dominant pattern)
**Secondary Influence:** Second-highest score (2+ occurrences)
**Mixed Pattern:** No score above 2 = Operating with conflicting belief systems

### Step 3: Belief Alignment Score Calculation

**Formula:**
```
Belief Alignment Score = (Healthy Operator responses / Total questions) × 100
```

- **80-100:** High alignment (beliefs support goals)
- **60-79:** Moderate alignment (some misalignment)
- **40-59:** Low alignment (significant belief-behavior gap)
- **0-39:** Critical misalignment (beliefs actively sabotaging goals)

### Step 4: Cross-Domain Consistency Check

**High Consistency (same pattern in 4+ domains):**
- Indicates deeply rooted belief system
- Harder to change but more predictable
- Clear intervention points

**Low Consistency (different patterns across domains):**
- Indicates contextual beliefs or recent shifts
- Easier to change but requires domain-specific approach
- May indicate transition period

## PERSONALIZED REPORT STRUCTURE

### Section 1: Your Primary Belief Pattern
- Pattern name and description

- Strengths of this pattern (when it serves you)

### Section 2: How This Shows Up in Your Life

- Patterns across career, money, relationships, health
- Behavioral signatures they'll recognize

### Section 3: The Hidden Cost
- What this belief pattern is costing them (opportunity cost)
- Estimated financial/time/energy impact
- Long-term trajectory if unchanged

### Section 4: The Gap Analysis
- Belief Alignment Score with interpretation
- Specific misalignments identified
- Stated values vs. operating beliefs

### Section 5: Your 30/60/90-Day Roadmap
- **30 Days:** Awareness practices specific to their pattern
- **60 Days:** Belief challenge exercises
- **90 Days:** New behavior implementation

### Section 6: Next Steps
- Immediate action: One belief to examine this week
- Resources for deeper work
- Soft introduction to IGNITE program (if relevant)

## A/B TESTING ROADMAP

### Test Variables
1. **Question order** - Does starting with emotional question vs. behavioral change completion?
2. **Answer options** - 4 vs. 5 options per question
3. **Results gating** - Full results before email vs. teaser then email
4. **Report delivery** - Immediate web display vs. email-only PDF
5. **Language tone** - Direct/challenging vs. supportive/empathetic

### Success Metrics
- **Completion rate:** Target 70%+ (typical: 50-60%)
- **Email conversion:** Target 50%+ (quiz visitors to email subscribers)
- **Time to complete:** Target 6-10 minutes
- **Engagement score:** Track which patterns share results most

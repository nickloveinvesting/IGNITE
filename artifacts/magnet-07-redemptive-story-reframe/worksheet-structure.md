## PSYCHOLOGICAL DESIGN PRINCIPLES APPLIED

**1. COMPLETION-PROBLEM EFFECT (Research-Backed)**
- Provide examples before asking users to complete exercises
- Pages 3-4 show completed redemptive stories (Jobs, Blakely, Disney, Oprah)
- This models the framework before users apply it themselves

**2. PROGRESS VISUALIZATION**
- Each prompt page shows "Prompt X of 5" with visual progress bar
- Celebrates completion: "âœ“ Prompt 1 Complete! You're 20% there."

**3. QUICK WINS FIRST**
- Prompt 1 (naming the experience) is easier than Prompt 5 (writing full story)
- Scaffolded difficulty builds momentum

**4. EXTERNALIZATION LANGUAGE**
- Prompts use "What happened to you" vs. "What did you do"
- Separates person from problem (narrative therapy principle)

**5. HOPEFUL, FORWARD-FOCUSED TONE**
- Design aesthetic is redemptive (sunrise, not darkness)
- Language emphasizes "who you're becoming" not "what happened to you"

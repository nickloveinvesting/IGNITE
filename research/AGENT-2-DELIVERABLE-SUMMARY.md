## ðŸ’¡ KEY RESEARCH FINDINGS

### What Makes Commitment Language Compelling

**The 7 Essential Elements:**
1. **Specificity** - "20 min, 3x/week" NOT "exercise more"
2. **Use "WILL"** - Not "should/might/can"
3. **Include Dates** - "By Nov 30, 2025" NOT "soon"
4. **Present Tense** - "I AM" for identity statements
5. **Personal Pronouns** - "I/Me/My" not "one should"
6. **Action Verbs** - "I commit" not "I hope"
7. **Positive Framing** - What TO do, not what to avoid

### Empowering vs. Shaming Language

**âŒ SHAME (Never Use):**
- "I'm lazy and a failure"
- "I am a quitter"
- Character attacks
- "I am a mistake"

**âœ… EMPOWERMENT (Always Use):**
- "I am capable of discipline"
- "I learned from past attempts"
- Behavior-focused
- "I made a mistake"

**Key Principle:** Shame paralyzes. Empowerment motivates.

### Psychological Principles That Work

**1. Written Commitments**
- Increase follow-through by 18%
- Physical writing > typing > thinking
- Creates psychological ownership

**2. Cialdini's Commitment & Consistency**
- People want to be consistent with past actions
- Small commitments lead to larger ones
- Public commitments stronger than private

**3. Identity-Based Change (James Clear)**
- "Incentives start habits, identity sustains them"
- Focus on WHO you're becoming, not WHAT you're achieving
- Every action is a "vote" for your identity

**4. Social Accountability (Burchard)**
- Public declarations put "ego on the line"
- Accountability partners increase success rates
- Community support reinforces commitment

**WINNERS do this:**
